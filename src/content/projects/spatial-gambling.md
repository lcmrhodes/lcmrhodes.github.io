This work focused on how spatial analysis can make public-health risk easier to read and reason about. I combined census-derived features, vulnerability indexing, and spatial regression to look at how gambling-related harm varied geographically.

The important part for this portfolio is the mindset behind it: visualisation was not an afterthought. The maps and spatial outputs were part of the argument, not just a way of decorating the final report.
